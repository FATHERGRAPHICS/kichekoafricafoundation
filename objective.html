Our Objectives

───────

Kicheko Africa Foundation

1:

To fight against gender harassment, humiliation and all forms of discrimination facing an employees in a work place.

We are dedicated to eradicating gender-based harassment, humiliation, and discrimination in the workplace. Through legal advocacy, awareness campaigns, and educational programs, we empower employees to understand and protect their rights.

2:

Promote education to the community.

Education is key to societal development. We actively work with schools, local authorities, and community leaders to ensure accessible education for all, particularly marginalized groups, so they can be active participants in societal change.

3:

Implement social and economic policies for societal welfare by involving community.

We collaborate with communities to develop and implement social and economic policies that support societal welfare, focusing on inclusivity and sustainable development. By doing so, we empower communities to be agents of their own change.

4:

Enhance opportunities for community through workshops and seminars locally and internationally.

We organize workshops and seminars both locally and internationally to provide individuals with the skills and knowledge necessary for personal and professional development. These forums also serve as platforms for addressing issues of discrimination and gender inequality.

5:

Exchange experiences and skills with global community organizations to fight for their rights.

By partnering with global community organizations, we exchange experiences, resources, and best practices to fight for the rights of the underrepresented. This global network allows us to bring diverse perspectives to our local communities while contributing to the broader fight for justice.

6:

Unite genders through workshops and forums for community to share their views and challenges.

We believe in the power of conversation and unity. Through workshops and forums, we create safe spaces where individuals of all genders can come together, share their experiences, discuss challenges, and collaborate on solutions that promote gender equality and social harmony.

7:

Protect Tanzanian and African culture.

Protecting and promoting Tanzanian and African culture is at the heart of our work. We are committed to ensuring that development does not come at the expense of our rich cultural heritage, but rather enhances it through thoughtful integration into modern society.
